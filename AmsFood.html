Café-Restaurant 't Heemelrijck

Café-Restaurant 't Heemelrijck on Van Woustraat, Amsterdam, is back. Since 1949, this family-run eatery has been dishing up classic Dutch fare like chowder and stew. Founded as a sandwich shop by Grandpa Nars, it evolved into a successful restaurant in 1964. In 1976, it became Amsterdam's first French bistro with a Dutch twist. In 2010, the second generation passed the torch, but the itch remained. Now, with the third generation, it's a new beginning at Van Woustraat 23

De silveren spiegel

The Oude Kerk is Amsterdam's oldest building, situated in the Amsterdam-Centrum district on Oudekerksplein. Initially dedicated to Saint Nicholas in 1306, it was known as St. Nicholas Church. After the Alteration in 1578, it ceased to be Roman Catholic. The current St. Nicholas Church in central Amsterdam was built in 1884-1887. In 2006, the 700th anniversary of Oude Kerk's use was celebrated, and a replica of the lost Angelus bell was reinstalled in 2016. Today, Oude Kerk hosts both ecclesiastical functions and site-specific exhibitions.

Restaurant Moeders

Moeders (= Dutch for mothers) opened in 1990. The owner asked all his guests to bring a plate, glass and cutlery during the opening. With all those different plates, wineglasses and cutlery the wooden tables in the restaurant are still set today.Today, 30 years since the opening, the walls in the restaurant are filled with hundreds of framed and unframed pictures along with antique pans, plates and biscuit tins. Moeders is known for several Dutch specialities. If you want to enjoy a typical Dutch treat they serve a Dutch ricedish. You don't get any rice, but all little bits and pieces of traditional Dutch home cooking. And for those who don't want to enjoy all of this, there are plenty of other things to chose from the extensive, international menu.

Restaurant Wilde-zwijnen

In April 2010 we opened our doors at Javaplein in Amsterdam East. For us, a Modern Dutch kitchen means: ingredients from Dutch soil, whereby the more local the ingredients come from, the better. We cook seasonal, vegetarian and use sustainable fish and game as much as possible.
